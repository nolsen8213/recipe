Grilled Bruschetta

Spring is around the corner, and what better motivation do you need to start preparing your summer garden than a bright and beautifl dish prepared with tomatoes fresh from the garden. There is nothing fancy about this recipe it simply relies on the great taste that a sun-bathed tomatoe can produce.

  • 2 lb assorted heirloom tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/4 + 2 tb extra virgin olive oil (so important it's great quality, certified extra virgin is the only way to go.
  • 3 tb red wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup packed basil leave, thinly sliced
  • kosher salt and fresh cracked black peper, to taste
  • 4 1/2 inch thick slices of ciabatta bread

put the tomatoes, onion, garlic, vinegar, 1/4 cup of olive oil, and the basil in a bowl and stir to combine. Season the mixture with salt and pepper and let sit at room temperature for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Heat a grill or grill pan over high heat. Grill the bread on each side until slightly charred, about 30 seconds per side. Remove from the grill and brush the tops with the remaining 2 tb of oil.

Mound the warm bread with the tomatoe mixture and some of the juices and enjoy!
